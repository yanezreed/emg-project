## Problem Statement

Due to the demands and intricacies of selling instrument parts online, a lot of the communication between customers and users involves repetitively answering recurring questions on item compatibility, availability, postage insurance, and delivery changes. For staff to be able to accurately respond to each conversation, they manually check the message history of the customer, search for item specifics, and craft this all in a consistent, professional, welcoming tone.

Being hyper-repetitive and having to traverse different programs and chrome tabs, this manual process is extremely prone to inconsistency. Leading to errors in replies and slower response times.

Introducing automation, I believe, could streamline the repetitive aspects of customer support at this level. Generating quality responses could allow staff to focus their time on selective complex inquiries, avoid errors forced by cumulative repetitive responses, and promote consistency by reducing workload induced stress.

So the absolute goal of this project is to automate only the repetitive elements of the customer support process, not to replace human supervision. Not replacing human judgment, but supporting it. As the business will still rely on staff's ability to assess responses, editing where is needed, to safeguard the quality and tone of customer relations.
